Frame Data

Every move for every character. New to frame data? Read the panel below — then let the colors do the work.

📖 How to read this

CommandThe buttons you press for the move (e.g. df+1).
HitWhere it hits: h=high (duckable), m=mid (block standing), l=low (block crouching).
StartupHow fast it comes out, in frames. Lower = faster (i10 beats i15).
BlockWhat happens to them after you block it. Big negative = they're punishable — your turn to hit back.
HitWhat happens after it hits you. Positive = they get to keep pressing.
CHCounter-hit value — bonus advantage if it interrupts your button.

The simple version: look at the Block column color. Green = it's safe / your turn after blocking. Yellow = mildly minus. Orange = you can launch them for blocking it. You don't need the numbers — the color tells you who's in danger.
