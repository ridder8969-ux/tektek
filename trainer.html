Defense

your biggest weakness — Defense 36

Your numbers say you mash into counter-hits and can't hold a turn. This section is your climb. Building Defense from 36 toward your Attack's 72 is the entire game for you.

1 · When in doubt, block
Hold b for mids/highs, hold db for lows. Most of your losses come from pressing when it's not your turn. Doing nothing is often the winning move.
2 · Stop pressing after YOUR blocked moves
When your move is negative (df1 is -3, db4 is -26), it's their turn. Don't mash a follow-up — that's how you eat counter-hits. Let go and block.
3 · Block the lows
Hold db. Lows hurt on hit but are usually launch-punishable on block. Block them, then punish from crouch (ws1 = rise+X launches).
4 · Always punish what you block
Blocking without punishing just hands them another turn. See the Punishers tab — 2,1 for small, F1+2 for big.
⚠ Throw breaks (your Throw Escape is C)

Throws are broken by matching the animation: 1+2 (X+Y), 1 (X), or 2 (Y). For now, default to mashing X+Y when grabbed — it breaks the most common throws. Refine by reading the arm animation later.

Panic moves — use SPARINGLY

These are last resorts, not habits. You already press too much. 1,1,1 (X,X,X) i10 interrupt but -12 · 2+3 (Y+A) power crush/armor but punishable · uf3 (uf+A) launches but -26 on block. They all lose to throws and patient blocking.

Universal Punishment

the skill that beats EVERY character

This is the real key to your climb — and it works against all 40 characters, so you don't need to memorize 40 frame charts. Almost everyone's unsafe moves fall into a few types. Learn to recognize the type, and your Lars punish is always the same.

The one rule that never changes

When you block something and the opponent is frozen / can't move for a beat — that was unsafe. Press your punish immediately. Small freeze → 2,1 (Y,X). Big freeze → F1+2 (f+X+Y) launch. You don't need to know the exact number — you need to recognize the freeze and react.

The 5 unsafe move TYPES everyone has

type 1
Launchers (on block)
Big single hits that launch on hit are almost always -13 to -15 on block. When you block a scary power move and they freeze → F1+2 to launch them back. Examples everyone has: hopkicks (uf4-type), big mids.
type 2
Hellsweeps & big lows
Sweeping lows that knock down are usually launch-punishable. Block low (hold db), then punish from crouch with ws1 (rise+X) to launch. This is how you stop Mishima hellsweep spam.
type 3
String enders
Long strings (like Hwoarang's) usually end unsafe — that's the trade for the pressure. Block the WHOLE string, punish the last hit with F1+2 or 2,1. Never interrupt the middle.
type 4
Whiffed pokes
When someone throws a move at the wrong range and it whiffs, the recovery is huge. Step back to bait it, then F1+2 or b3,4 into the empty space. Whiff punishing is a core win condition.
type 5
"Safe" pressure (-9 to -10)
Lots of moves are only slightly minus. You can't launch, but you can still take your turn with 2,1 (Y,X) or just press df1 (df+X) to check them. Don't sit and eat another turn.

Lost to a character you don't know? Go to Practice, set the CPU to that character, and just block everything. Watch which moves leave them frozen — those are your punish targets. vs Hwoarang your worst matchup

Hwoarang's whole game is long kick strings that bait you into mashing. When you press mid-string, he counter-hits you and keeps his turn. The counter is the most disciplined thing in Tekken: block, and do nothing.

step 1
Block the whole string
Hold b for the mid kicks, db when you see a low. Let the entire string finish on block. Do NOT try to interrupt — that's the bait.
step 2
Punish the end
Most of his strings end very unsafe. The moment his kicks stop and he's frozen → launch with F1+2 f+X+Y, or 2,1 YX if only slightly unsafe.
don't
Don't interrupt mid-string
Unless you KNOW a specific gap (you don't yet), pressing during his string loses. Interrupting is exactly how you lose this matchup.
do
Respect lows, launch the bad ones
Hold db and watch for low-kick animations. When you block an unsafe low, punish from crouch with ws1 (rise+X) to launch.
✓ Say this out loud while you play
Block the string · Punish the end · Don't press

That one sentence flips this matchup at your rank.

vs Bryan the discipline test

Bryan is the character that punishes exactly the habit your stats flagged — mashing into counter-hits. He has no fast mid launcher and a bad low game, so he wins by making you press into his huge counter-hit buttons. Beat Bryan by slowing down. This is the matchup that builds your defense.

Understand his whole game in one idea

Bryan's damage comes from counter-hits and whiff punishes, not from opening you up. His jab strings and pokes are designed to bait a button so he lands a CH launcher. If you stop mashing and just block, Bryan has a genuinely hard time opening you up. Patience starves him.

His big threats — what's hurting you

launch-punishable low
Snake Edge — df+3
His famous sweeping low that launches on hit — low-rank Bryans spam it. It's slow (i29) so you can block it on reaction by holding db. On block it's -26 → free launch. Block low → ws1 (rise + X) to launch him. This single habit wins games vs Bryan.
the "deathfist"
Hatchet / f,F+2
A fast, long-range high that's a Heat Engager and does big damage. It's -9 on block — not quite launchable, but it's a high, so you can duck it if you read it and launch with ws1. At minimum, block it (hold b) and take your turn with 2,1.
don't challenge
qcb+1 — his keepout button
+5 on block and a counter-hit launcher. This is his best button and he'll throw it constantly at mid-range. Do not press after blocking it — it's his turn (+5). Respect it, hold block, wait for your moment. Mashing here is how you eat a combo.
the bait
Jab strings — 1,2,x
His jabs have huge range. 1,2 is his offense starter; the 1,2,1 third hit is a CH launcher AND safe, so he's daring you to interrupt. 1,2,3 ends in a low (-12, punishable), 1,2,4 ends high (Heat Engager). Block the string, don't interrupt — then punish the ender.

vs Dragunov the rushdown bully

Dragunov is relentless pressure with great pokes, plus-frames, and a scary throw game. But his offense is more bark than bite once you stop respecting it — most of his turns end and can be interrupted, and his big lows are launch-punishable. Keys: duck his key strings, step his approach, break throws.

His whole game in one idea

He chips you with pokes (d+2, df+1, jabs) and plus-frames, then cashes out with throws or a counter-hit. His launching offense is thin outside WR2. Block patiently, duck the right strings, and he gives his turn back constantly.

His big threats

step it left
WR2 (f,f,F+2) — his approach
His main neutral tool to rush in. Players throw it mindlessly. Sidestep LEFT to make it whiff, then whiff-punish with F1+2. A backdash also makes his throws fall short.
DUCK these
b+4,2,1 and d+4,1
These must be ducked — the last hits are high. Block standing all day and he frame-traps you. Duck the high ending and launch. The #1 Dragunov-specific read.
launch on block
His lows — d+3+4, db+3+4
Launch-punishable on block. Hold db, block low, punish from crouch with ws1 (rise+X). When he throws one up close, make him pay.
break them
His throw game
F+1+4, F+2+3, uf+1+2. Default to breaking 1+2 (X+Y) and watch the arm. A sidestep or backdash makes throw attempts whiff entirely.
✓ Say this vs Dragunov
Step left on approach · Duck b4,2,1 & d4,1 · Break throws

vs Jin the fundamentalist

Jin is a clean, fundamentals character — strong pokes, a parry, a counter-hit game, electric. No cheap gimmick; he beats you with solid play. So beat him the same way: block, punish his unsafe stuff, don't feed his counter-hits.

His big threats

respect it
df+1 and ff+2
df+1 is his strong safe poke that starts pressure; ff+2 is his safe Heat-engaging mid he throws constantly. Both safe — don't try to punish, just block (hold b) and take your turn when it's yours.
don't mash
CH launchers (f+4, etc.)
Jin has strong counter-hit buttons that launch if you press into them. His mid-range wants you mashing. Stay patient — block and whiff-punish rather than challenging.
DUCK this
2,1,4 string
Commonly spammed at round start. The ending is duckable/punishable on block. Don't respect the whole thing — block it and punish the unsafe ender with F1+2.
don't get parried
Kazama parry (b+1+3)
Jin can parry attacks. Don't throw predictable single highs into a reading Jin. Vary timing — and remember throws beat parries, so your 1+2 throw punishes a parry-happy Jin.

His big threats

bait it

vs King the grappler

King is the throw monster — grabs and chain throws that delete half your health. At your rank he wins mostly because people don't break throws. A defense and throw-break test. His movement is bad and many strikes are slow, so once you handle grabs, you bully him.

⚠ The thing you MUST learn: throw breaks

Most King throws break on the hand he leads with: left hand → 1 (X), right → 2 (Y), both → 1+2 (X+Y). Giant Swing is a 1 (X) break even though it looks two-armed — when swung, mash 1 (X) to tech-roll and cut the damage. You won't break them all (nobody does), but breaking ~half flips the matchup.

His big threats & your answers

launch on block
Atlas Hammer & his snake-edge low
Atlas Hammer is unsafe — always launch-punish with F1+2. His slow snake-edge low launches on hit; hold db, block low, punish (ws1) or low-parry.
punish it
df+1 extension & df+2,1
His df+1 extension is -10 → jab-punish (2,1 = Y,X). Duck df+2,1 if he fishes. His foot power-crush is ~-10 — jab punish that too.
do this
Check him with pokes, step throws
Up close, smother him with df1 (df+X) and jabs — his strikes are slow, so you interrupt. Sidestep throw attempts; many whiff if you're moving.
your edge
Bully him, bait the grab
King wants you passive so he can grab. Stay active and counter-hit when you read a throw — a quick button beats grab startup. Pressure his bad defense.
✓ Say this vs King

vs Eddy the capoeira flowchart

The ultimate "looks confusing, is actually a flowchart" character. Endless spinning kicks from his stances, lots of lows and highs, designed to overwhelm you into mashing or freezing. His whole game in one idea

Eddy throws fast flashy strings that look unblockable but are mostly highs/lows you block by alternating b / db. He wins by making you guess wildly. Slow down, block low when in doubt (lots of his pressure is lows), punish the gaps. Don't out-spin him.

⚠ Two honest limits

It can't update itself. A file on your device can't pull live patch data — that's why updates come through me. Saved checks are per-file-location. When you swap in a new copy I send, the checkboxes may reset (the matchup content is what matters, not the checks). Small price for a thing that just works with zero setup.
